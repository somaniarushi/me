## ‚õèÔ∏è ~~Cosplaying~~ Entrepreneurship
In freshman year, I started a doomed startup with my friends. It was called VATT, with the mission to make audio-visual content on the internet language agnostic through automatic dubbing and subtitling. We sold it for half a grand in the end. It wasn't really a company. But it was a wonderful experience.

I spent a lot of time in college helping campus startups. This was through the Berkeley [Gateway to Innovation](https://begin.berkeley.edu) program, the [Student Entrepreneurship](https://step.berkeley.edu) program, [Cal Hacks Fellowship](https://fellowship.calhacks.io) and the [DRF Female Founders Track](https://femalefounders.dormroomfund.com/). Most startups are doomed, and that was the case with most startups I worked with, but that doesn't mean they shouldn't exist. Most of the process of starting something is just trying many, many times.
